About

I'm a first-year PhD student in Mechanical Engineering at Rochester Institute of Technology, working as a Graduate Research Assistant in the Flow Physics & Modeling Lab (FPML) under the supervision of Prof. Qian Xue.

My work sits at the intersection of fluid mechanics, bio-inspired engineering, and artificial intelligence — using computational modeling and machine learning to understand how biological systems sense and move through fluids, and translating those principles into engineered sensors and designs.

Research Interests

Passive and active fluid–structure–acoustics interaction, neuromuscular biomechanics, and machine-learning-aided modeling of complex biological flows.

🐋

Bio-Inspired Design

Learning from nature's sensors and structures — like seal whiskers — to design engineered systems that detect and adapt to their fluid environment.

🌊

Fluid–Structure Interaction

Modeling how deformable structures and surrounding flows couple and exchange energy, from vortex-induced vibration to flow sensing.

🧠

Machine Learning for Physics

Physics-informed and operator-learning methods (e.g., DeepONets) to accelerate and enrich high-fidelity simulation of multiphysics systems.

📡

Sensor Design

Bio-inspired flow and acoustic sensing — translating biological sensing strategies into robust, real-world sensor arrays.
