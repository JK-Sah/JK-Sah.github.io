About

I'm J.K. Sah, a PhD student in Mechanical Engineering at Rochester Institute of Technology, working as a Graduate Research Assistant in the Flow Physics & Modeling Lab under Dr. Qian Xue.

My work is hands-on and experimental: I design, build, and test embedded sensor arrays and data-acquisition systems for autonomous underwater detection and tracking, inspired by how seals use their whiskers to sense wakes. I pair towing-tank flow experiments with machine-learning analysis of sensor data to decode whisker–wake interaction.

Research

Experimental fluid–structure interaction and bio-inspired flow sensing — from sensor hardware and towing-tank experiments to the machine-learning pipelines that turn raw signals into motion state.

🦭

Bio-Inspired Flow Sensing

Translating how seals sense hydrodynamic trails with their whiskers into engineered sensor arrays for underwater detection and tracking.

🔧

Embedded Sensor Arrays

A 32-channel whisker-array DAQ system (24-bit ΔΣ ADCs over SPI), released as a Python package for synchronized logging and visualization.

🌊

Whisker–Wake Interaction

Towing-tank experiments and a frequency-domain pipeline that separates whisker self-vibration from wake-induced forcing.

🧠

Machine Learning for Physics

CNN–LSTM models mapping multi-channel deflection signals to hydrodynamic motion state; physics-informed neural operators (DeepONet).
